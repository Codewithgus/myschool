philosophy of mary help of christians catholic high school

the mary help of christians catholic high school primaryly exists for the of sharing with the students the principle of christians education which aims at the formation of human persons with respect to their ultimate goal, and simultaneosly with respect to the good of society of which as persons they are members and in whose responsibilities ,as adults the share.

vision statement

mary help of christians catholic high school exists primaryly for the purpose of giving to the students the principles of values. included in these objectives's is to aid the students in their growth towards full maturity;to make them aware of their capabilities and limitations, thus enabling them to meet life's challenges.

mission statement

to be an institution of learners and professionals with a vision and a vibrant faith in jesus christ, the master teacher, and in the church's magisterium. to strive here and now,collaborating with the family, the community and the church to lift the veil of ignorance and to impart gospel values of love of god and neighbor the value of peace which flows from justice,the values of gratitude and respect of this our universe and for the gifts it contains. to serve as leaven in a world stuggling to know god and thereby to serve him,a world struggling for intergrity and wounded by selfishnes ,immorality and injustice.

the catholic schools

like other catholic schools in the archdiocese of monrovia mhccs operates under the umbrella of the catholic education secretariat(ces),but is recognized as a private school owned and run by the salesians and with the educative style of saint john bosco.

the catholic schools are the property of the catholic church and are places where love and respect for humankind is developed,natured and brought to maturity;where knowledge is shared and where creativity and personal initiatives are encouraged and supported. therefore the catholic schools welcome all who search for knowledge imbused with christian values, principle and pratices.

the following aspects of the culture which promote human dignity are emphasized in all the catholic schools:

  1. the buildings are to be well lighted,ventilated and maintained.
  2. classrooms are to be well equipped.
  3. there should be adequate space for offices.
  4. science labs are to be well equipped.
  5. libraires are to be functional.
  6. toilet facilities must be adequate.
  7. the classrooms, the buildings and the campus as a whole must be kept clean always.
  8. the classrooms are to be cleaned bt the students and not by the janitors.
  9. the teachers and students are to be educated to be take care of the buildings,equipments,furniture at all times.
  10. the teachers/educators should be present amoung their pupils as brothers/sisters and father and mothers especially during the recess period.

the background of mary help of christians catholic school

mary help of christians catholic high school was founded by the salesians of don bosco and opened officially on the 17 th november 2003. it began its first lessons in the rooms located in don bosco youth centre during the war. the salesian community knew the great value of a catholic school in matadi for the needs of the neighborhood youth. this dream came true also through the encouragement and support of the parishioners of holy innocent parish,new matadi-estate.

fr.blamoh harris, the first principal of the school, in accordance with the regualtion of ces, hired teachers and received the needed suppot then from the ces. the first graduation exercise took place in november 2013.

administration

in a catholic institution of learning, administration is characterized by service,responsibily,loyalty borne out of love and respect for others,for the institution and for the church

administration must create suitable working and learning condition for all concerned and within the frame work of these policies make necessary changes and adjustments or addition which will contribute to the smooth runnign of the institution.

the salesian community is the overall administrative organ and is prsent in the day-to-day activities of the institute through the principal. the principal will constuture the administrative board.

the administrative team consists of the principal,the vice-principal(s),the finance officer,registrar and the fean of students the administration team is chaired by the principal and is cited to meeting by him. the administration should be able to meet at least once in two weeks when special need arises, after the administrative team meeting(s), if there are decisions to bemade based on the meeting, the final word rests on the principal in the decision making through he seeks to get the support of the rest fo the administrative team members.

the teaching staff

the staff in catholic schools are called to exercise professionalism at all times in the spirit of christian witness, the staff should at all times be a model of good moral behavior both intellectual and moral,expect form the staff understanding,patience,commitment and love. the teaching staff should not manipulate,abuse or destroy the innocence of our children. they must not be manipulated or exploited financially,sexually or otherwise. each student is unique and must be treated as such. a staff in mary help of christians catholic school is expected to conduct him/herself becomingly in the campus and outside of it.

the teaching staff is expected to prepare his/her lessons and impart the same with deligence,patience and love. they must be models of human virtues like honesty,commitment,moral uprightness,understanding,obedience,patience,humility,decency,truthfulness. we put much emphasis on their well-being and development in all aspects of human existence. administrators and teaching staff who do not love,respect,honor,accept,and protect our children as children of god and look at these children as a gifts from god to them have no place in the catholic schools. the students are God's own and ours.

the students

the catholic schools are places where we bring up our children well morally,spiritually and intellectually. the rationale behind the existence of our schools is nothing but the children who come to our schools.

students are required to be good,respectful,obedient,and hardworking and are expected to carry out the tasks given them. we expect the best of their intellectual performances,and to be studious and determined to carry out their academics tasks. they must be able to meet the demands of academics and co-curricular activities. they are expected to be punctual and self-disciplined.

chain of command

it is necessary that the students, the parents and the staff know the chain of command and be governed accordingly. in dealing with problems involving students the following procedures must be followed:

  • the subject teacher
  • the sponsor
  • the dean of students
  • the vice-principal for instructions/or any other administrators
  • the principal

any misunderstanding among/between teachers must be handled by the dean of students/one of the administrators. generally,the principal should be the last person to get involved in solving the problem. the teachers/other employees of the institution are not allowed to go to catholic secretariat or any other authorities by-passing the school administration especially the principal.
